Smart Campus Interface

Unified control surfaces for classrooms and campus-wide digital signage for visual communication, wayfinding, and emergency alerts.

Overview

Every day, Auburn faculty walk into classrooms facing complex AV systems, uncertain how to start recordings or switch inputs. Meanwhile, visitors wander campus looking for buildings, students miss event announcements, and gameday crowds struggle to find parking. Smart Campus Interface solves both challenges with a unified approach.

In classrooms, Stream Deck control surfaces replace confusing touch panels with intuitive, visual buttons‚Äîone press to record, one press for lights, one press for help. Across campus, a network of digital displays delivers real-time information exactly where people need it: schedules at building entrances, parking availability at lot entrances, shuttle ETAs at transit stops, and emergency alerts everywhere.

Both systems connect to Auburn's Q-SYS backbone, enabling IT to manage every classroom button and campus display from a single dashboard. Configuration changes push instantly. Status monitoring happens in real-time. Support requests route automatically. The result: faculty focus on teaching, visitors find their way, and fans enjoy a seamless gameday experience.

Classroom Control Features

One-Touch Recording

Start and stop lecture recording with a single button press. Visual confirmation shows recording status at a glance.

Preset Lighting Scenes

Switch between presentation mode, discussion mode, and video playback lighting with dedicated buttons.

Video Conferencing

Quick-launch Zoom, Teams, or other platforms with pre-configured room settings already applied.

Integrated Help Request

One button sends an alert to IT/AV support with room information, enabling rapid response to issues.

Source Selection

Easily switch between laptop, document camera, and room PC inputs without navigating complex menus.

Remote Updates

IT staff can push button configuration changes to all devices campus-wide without visiting each room.

Integration

Both classroom control and digital signage connect to the Q-SYS ecosystem for centralized management. Stream Deck buttons trigger complex automation sequences while appearing simple to faculty. Digital signage content can be synchronized with audio announcements and automatically switches to emergency mode when alerts are triggered. The unified content management system integrates with Auburn's room scheduling, transit tracking, and event management systems.
