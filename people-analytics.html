People Analytics

Intelligent tracking for safety, operations, and facility optimization while respecting privacy.

Overview

People Analytics uses anonymous counting and flow analysis to help Auburn optimize space utilization, improve safety, and enhance the campus experience. By understanding how people move through buildings and outdoor spaces, the university can make data-driven decisions about scheduling, staffing, and facility planning.

Importantly, this system focuses on aggregate counts and flow patterns‚Äînot individual identification. Privacy-by-design principles ensure compliance with FERPA and university policies while still delivering actionable insights.

Technology Options

Thermal Counting Sensors

Detect body heat signatures to count entries and exits. No visual identification‚Äîjust anonymous counts.

Stereo Vision Sensors

3D depth sensors count people by detecting movement and size without capturing identifiable images.

LiDAR Systems

Laser-based detection provides highly accurate counts for large spaces and outdoor areas.

WiFi Analytics

Aggregate analysis of connected device density provides zone-level occupancy estimates.

Privacy & Compliance

üîí Privacy-First Design

  • No facial recognition or individual identification
  • Only aggregate counts and flow patterns collected
  • Data anonymized at the sensor level before transmission
  • Compliant with FERPA student privacy requirements
  • No integration with student records or ID systems
  • Transparent signage informs occupants of counting sensors
  • Data retention policies limit storage duration
  • Regular privacy audits ensure ongoing compliance
